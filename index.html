Our Menu

Tagine

A slow-cooked stew made with spiced vegetables and meat, chicken, or fish. The ingredients are placed inside heavy pottery vessels called “tagines” and cooked until the meat is tender and falls off the bone. The dish is served with bread to mop up the flavorful broth.

Couscous

A staple food in Morocco, couscous is a type of pasta made from semolina flour. It is steamed and served with meat, vegetables, and a spicy sauce. Couscous is often eaten on Fridays after prayers and at special occasions such as weddings and funerals.

Harira

A traditional soup made with tomatoes, lentils, chickpeas, and spices such as ginger, cinnamon, and saffron. Harira is often served during Ramadan to break the fast at sunset.
